Air Quality Hits Severe Levels

On December 13, 2025, Delhi’s Air Quality Index (AQI) breached the 400 mark, slipping into the “severe” category. The Commission for Air Quality Management (CAQM) immediately enforced Stage III of the Graded Response Action Plan (GRAP), banning construction and demolition activities across the NCR.

Impact on Daily Life

  • Thick smog reduced visibility and caused breathing discomfort for residents.
  • Doctors warned that children, senior citizens, and asthma patients face higher risks.
  • Schools up to grade 5 were closed as part of emergency measures.

Restrictions Under GRAP-III

The restrictions include a ban on non-essential construction, stone crushers, brick kilns, and BS-III petrol/BS-IV diesel vehicles. Transport of construction materials on unpaved roads has also been halted.

Local Emissions Driving Crisis

Recent analysis shows that local emissions—from vehicles, industries, and everyday activities—are now the primary drivers of Delhi’s winter smog, even as farm fires have declined. Smaller NCR towns like Ghaziabad and Bahadurgarh are experiencing intense smog, highlighting the expanding pollution hotspots.
